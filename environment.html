Farming insects requires
far fewer natural resources
than traditional livestock.

As the world population increases the livestock industry puts greater and greater strain on the earth’s natural resources. Farming insects could be the key to alleviating some of this strain.

13x less farm land

Due to advancements in vertical farming techniques crickets can be grown inside of multistory buildings. Unlike cows which need many acres or land to graze.

5x less feed

Because insects are cold blooded they can more efficiently convert food to body weight. That means eating insects has an environmental impact closer to plants than animals

1000x less water

A cow drinks a lot of water in it’s lifetime. Because crickets reach adulthood much quicker they require far less water
