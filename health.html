Insects are high in protein and packed with essential nutrients

One of the main reasons that insects are so good for us is because we usually consume the entire organism not just the muscle tissue. That means we get 100% of the nutrients. Try doing that with a chicken!

Complete protein

While the nutritional profiles range from insect to insect, many are complete protein sources. This means they contain all the essemntial amino acids needed by the human body.

Good source of calcium

Calcium is the most abundant mineral in the human body and it’s important to consume enough of it to maintain healthy bones. When we eat insects we actually consume their entire skeleton system (exoskeleton) making them a wonderful source of calcium.

Mealworms have more omega-3 than salmon

Omega-3 fatty acids are an essential nutrient for normal metabolism and brain function. It has been shown to reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease and prevent cancer. Salmon is known for being a great source of omega-3 but mealworms actually contain more!

THE IMPORTANCE OF DHA

This omega-3, found primarily in animal foods, is the primary structural component of the human brain, cerebral cortex, skin, sperm, testicles and retina. It has been showed to prevent Alzheimer's and reduce the risk of childhood brain developent issues.
